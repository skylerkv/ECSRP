Covid 19

COVID-19, caused by the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2, emerged in late 2019 and quickly evolved into a global pandemic, profoundly impacting societies worldwide. This highly contagious respiratory
illness has presented unprecedented challenges, leading to widespread illness, loss of life, and significant disruptions to daily life. Governments and health organizations have implemented
various measures such as lockdowns, social distancing, and vaccination campaigns to curb the spread of the virus. The pandemic has not only strained healthcare systems but also exposed and
exacerbated social and economic inequalities. Efforts to combat COVID-19 continue through international collaboration, scientific advancements, and ongoing public health initiatives, highlighting
the collective resilience and adaptability of communities in the face of a complex global health crisis.
